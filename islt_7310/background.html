Background

Required Courses

  • 7301 Introduction to Information Technology 3 credits Fall 2018

    Explains and introduces information technology including the information cycle, societal transfer of information and the role of technology in information transfer.

  • 7302 Organization of Information 3 credits Fall 2019

    Lays the foundation for the process of organizing information including details regarding catalogs, metadata, and indexing records.

  • 7305 Foundations of Library & Information Science 3 credits Fall 2018

    Introduces the history, development, guiding principles and values in the field of library and information science providing foundational theories, historical perspectives and current practices.

  • 7313 Collection & Access Management 3 credits Fall 2019

    Explores and educates future professionals about the work of managing, compiling, maintaining, weeding, and preserving collections and technological access to print and digital records.

  • 7314 Reference Sources & Services 3 credits Spring 2019

    Introduces reference sources and equips future information professionals with reference service skills to employ in user-based information agencies.

  • 7315 Management of Information Agencies 3 credits Spring 2019

    Applies principles and methods of management to information agencies and libraries.

Practicum

  • 7381 Practicum in Information Agencies 3 credits Fall 2021

    Provides practical work experience at a library or information agency supervised by a degreed professional in the field.

Elective courses

  • 7310 Web Design & Development 3 credits Spring 2020

    Introduces beginning HTML5 and CSS3 coding to teach the basics of computer web design and how to publish a website.

  • 9452 Information Literacy & Instruction 3 credits Spring 2020

    Develops knowledge of information literacy history, concepts, learning theories, effective teaching methods and practical application for instruction in libraries and information agencies.

  • 9443 The Academic Library 3 credits Summer 2021

    Introduces the academic library values, guiding principles, theories, practices, collections, mabnagement and future roles within the academic and institutional culture of the university.

  • 9450 Introduction to Research in Info. Science & Learning Tech. 3 credits Fall 2020

    Teaches the foundations of scholarly research methods and topics, how those are applied to the field of information science, and the process of scholarly communications in research.

  • 9454 Copyright in Libraries 3 credits Fall 2020

    Provides a working knowledge and practical skills of copyright law, ethical concerns, and copyright use within libraries.

  • 9409 Digital Libraries 3 credits Spring 2021

    Explores digital libraries and their role in today’s information environment to prepare students to implement and contribute to current and future digital libraries.

Adapted from: University of Missouri School of Information Science & Learning Technologies [SISLT] (2020a). Courses: Full list of SISLT courses. Retrieved from https://sislt.missouri.edu/courses/
