Background

My Degree background:  This page contains the requirements for completing University of Missouri's Master of Library & Information Science (MLIS) degree. The degree is composed of twelve credits from required courses, fifteen credits from elective courses (above 9000 level) and a three credit practicum. All courses listed here are each three credits.

Practicum

  • 7381 Practicum in Information Agencies     Fall 2021

    Provides practical work experience at a library or information agency supervised by a degreed professional in the field.

Required Courses

  • 7301 Introduction to Information Technology     Fall 2018

    Explains and introduces information technology including the information cycle, societal transfer of information and the role of technology in information transfer.

  • 7302 Organization of Information     Fall 2019

    Lays the foundation for the process of organizing information including details regarding catalogs, metadata, and indexing records.

  • 7305 Foundations of Library & Information Science     Fall 2018

    Introduces the history, development, guiding principles and values in the field of library and information science by exploring foundational theories, historical perspectives and current practices.

  • 7313 Collection & Access Management     Fall 2019

    Educates future information professionals about the work of managing, compiling, maintaining, weeding, and preserving collections and technological access to print and digital records.

  • 7314 Reference Sources & Services     Spring 2019

    Introduces reference sources and equips future information professionals with reference service skills to employ in user-based information agencies.

  • 7315 Management of Information Agencies     Spring 2019

    Applies principles and methods of management to information agencies and libraries.

Elective courses
Number TitleSemester         
7310 Web Design & Development Spring 2020
Introduces beginning HTML5 and CSS3 coding. Teaches the basics of computer web design and how to publish a website.
9452 Information Literacy & Instruction Spring 2020
Develops knowledge of information literacy history, concepts, learning theories, effective teaching methods and practical applications of instruction in libraries and information agencies.
9443 The Academic Library Summer 2020
Introduces the academic library values, guiding principles, theories, practices, collections, and management. Places these within the institutional culture of the university and discusses future roles.
9450 Introduction to Research in Info. Science & Learning Tech. Fall 2020
Teaches the foundations of scholarly research methods and topics, how those are applied to the field of information science, and the process of scholarly communications in research.
9454 Copyright in Libraries Fall 2020
Provides working knowledge of and practical skills for copyright laws, ethical concerns, and copyright uses within libraries.
9409 Digital Libraries Spring 2021
Explores digital libraries and their role in today’s information environment to prepare students to implement and contribute to current and future digital libraries.
