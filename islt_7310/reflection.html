Reflection

Personal Reflection Statement

I believe in the power of libraries. Libraries connect people, teach literacy, strengthen communities, and promote lifelong learning. As I seek to obtain a Master of Library & Information Science degree, I am gaining valuable knowledge about historical, foundational, and current theories and practices. I am learning valuable technology skills to help libraries adapt and contribute in a global digital environment. My goal is to apply the skills and knowledge I am learning to contribute and improve libraries and information science agencies.

Professional Competencies

Determined, dedicated graduate student developing the ability to apply foundational, historical theories to current practice in libraries. Service-oriented, eager to learn, dedicated to helping libraries promote information literacy, strengthen community/student engagement, and develop a passion for lifelong learning. Key assignments completed include building tutorials, developing and delivering information literacy instruction, collaborating to create a collection development policy, creating thesaurus entries, grant-writing, and more.
